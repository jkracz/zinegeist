# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Writers (primary).** Independent digital writers looking for a noise-free home for their work — people escaping AI-generated feeds, doomscrolling, and the cluttered, growth-optimized surfaces of other platforms. They want somewhere their writing can live with a real URL they can return to, without renting space inside a tool meant for something else (Notion pages, Drive docs, personal sites cobbled together). Some are building an ongoing body of work; others just want to put one piece up and have it exist.

**Readers (initial).** Mostly other writers already on the platform, browsing to see what people are publishing — a small, curious shelf-browsing audience, not a mass-feed audience. The reading occasion is closer to picking something off a shelf than scrolling.

## Product Purpose

Zinegeist is a publishing home for independent writers — a place where writing lives, with a permanent URL, a small shelf you can grow, and a reading surface that treats the work as the point. Think of it as a CMS for indie writer publications: hosted, opinionated, and shaped around the act of publishing rather than the act of distributing.

Success means writers feel like they have a real place (not a workaround), and readers feel like they're browsing a shelf, not consuming a feed.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, clear, cozy.** A first-time visitor should leave thinking *this is where writing lives* — a place that's quiet, considered, and welcoming, with a slight handmade warmth. Voice is plainspoken and unhurried; never breathless, never marketing-coded, never apologetic about being small.

## Anti-references

- **Notion / Google Drive shared links.** Generic tooling repurposed as a publishing surface. Zinegeist should never feel like a workspace product where writing happens to live; the surface itself has to feel like a publication, not a document.
- **Personal-site builders (Squarespace/Wix/Carrd templates).** Zinegeist isn't trying to be your homepage with a writing section — it's a shared shelf of independent publications with consistent reading affordances.
- **Goodreads (the current version).** The *idea* of a community of readers around independent work is right; the cluttered, ad-laden, abandoned-shareware execution is the anti-reference. Aim for what a thoughtfully maintained Goodreads-for-writing could feel like.
- **Algorithmic feed / growth-hacker dashboards.** No engagement metrics displayed as the point, no follower-count theater, no "grow your audience" framing. Discovery is editorial-shelf shaped, not feed-shaped.

Avoid naming specific newsletter platforms as anti-references — newsletter delivery may live here in the future, and the platform shouldn't be defined in opposition to that medium.

## Design Principles

1. **Writing is the artifact.** Every surface defers to the work. Chrome, controls, and metadata recede; the type does the heavy lifting. If a UI element competes with a paragraph, the paragraph wins.
2. **A shelf, not a feed.** Discovery is finite, browsable, and editorially shaped. Counts, scores, and trending signals don't appear unless they help a reader find something worth reading.
3. **A real place, not a workaround.** The publishing surfaces should feel like a publication — paper warmth, considered typography, permanent URLs that read like addresses. Never a generic SaaS shell with writing pasted into it.
4. **Quiet by default, warm on contact.** Restraint at the system level (tinted neutrals, generous whitespace, low chroma) with small handmade gestures (paper grain, serif italics for emphasis, mono eyebrows) that signal care without performance.
5. **Practice what we preach.** No AI-generated copy, no doomscroll patterns, no dark UX, no growth-hacker language. The product enacts its own pitch — a writer reading the marketing and a writer using the editor should feel the same hand at work.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline. Sensible defaults: meet contrast on body and UI text in both themes, respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for ambient animations (scroll hints, paper-grain shimmer, hero motion), keep body type at a comfortable reading size (16px floor), and ensure all interactive controls are keyboard-reachable with visible focus rings using the existing `--ring` token.
